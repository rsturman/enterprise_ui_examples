A message from our CEO

Our people, our purpose, our future

When I walk through any of our 2,400 stores around the world, I see the same thing — teams of people who genuinely care about the customers and communities they serve. That spirit is not something you can manufacture or mandate. It grows from a shared sense of purpose, and it is the single greatest advantage Meridian & Co. possesses.

Our world is changing rapidly. Customers expect more transparency about where products come from and how they are made. They want convenience, but not at the cost of their values. Communities are asking businesses to be active partners in addressing social and environmental challenges. And our own team members rightly expect a workplace that invests in their growth and wellbeing.

"We are not simply a retailer that sells products. We are a connector — linking the people who make things with the people who need them, and doing so in a way that benefits everyone in between."

This intranet exists to make our strategic direction visible and accessible to every member of the Meridian family. Our mission, vision, and values are not abstract statements written for annual reports — they are working tools that should guide decisions at every level of this organisation, from the boardroom to the shop floor.

Our five-year strategy, Meridian Forward 2030, asks us to grow responsibly, invest in digital capability, deepen our commitment to sustainable sourcing, and above all, stay relentlessly focused on the communities we serve. These are ambitious goals. But I have seen what this organisation can achieve when it moves together with clarity and conviction.

Thank you for the work you do every day. I hope you will take the time to explore the pages that follow, reflect on how your role connects to our wider purpose, and share your ideas on how we can continue to improve.

Amara Osei

Our Mission

To provide quality, affordable products that improve everyday life — sourced responsibly, sold fairly, and delivered with care.

Our mission defines what we do today. It is the promise we make to every customer who walks through our doors or shops online. It shapes our buying decisions, our pricing strategy, and the experience we create in store and at home.

Our Vision

To become the world's most trusted retailer — known for putting people and planet at the heart of every decision we make.

Our vision describes the future we are building toward. It is deliberately aspirational. Trust is earned over decades and lost in moments, which is why it demands constant vigilance across everything from supply chain ethics to data privacy.

Understanding the difference

Mission vs. Vision

A mission statement explains the fundamental reason an organisation exists. It describes the present — what the company does, who it serves, and how it creates value. Meridian's mission centres on three interconnected promises: quality and affordability for customers, responsible sourcing from suppliers, and an experience delivered with genuine care.

A vision statement looks ahead. It articulates the long-term aspiration that the organisation is working toward. Meridian's vision to become the world's most trusted retailer sets a clear North Star that shapes strategic priorities and investment decisions. Whereas the mission can be measured through daily operations, the vision is a destination that drives continuous improvement.

Together, mission and vision form the strategic foundation of an organisation. The mission provides operational focus; the vision provides directional ambition. Effective strategies bridge the gap between the two.

What we stand for

Our four values

Values shape culture, and culture shapes performance. These four principles guide how we behave, how we make decisions, and how we treat every person we interact with — from suppliers and partners to customers and each other.

01

Customer First

Every decision starts with the customer. We listen before we act, we solve problems before they are reported, and we measure our success by the loyalty and satisfaction of the people we serve.

02

Act with Integrity

We do the right thing even when no one is watching. Transparency in pricing, honesty with suppliers, and ethical conduct across our supply chain are non-negotiable commitments we make to every stakeholder.

03

Sustainable Growth

Growth without responsibility is not growth — it is debt. We pursue commercial success in ways that regenerate communities, protect the environment, and create long-term value for future generations.

04

Stronger Together

We believe diversity makes us better, collaboration makes us faster, and respect makes us stronger. We invest in our people because their growth is inseparable from ours.

Strategic Direction 2025 – 2030

Meridian Forward 2030

Our five-year strategy sets out how we will bridge the gap between our mission today and our vision for the future. It is built on five strategic pillars — each reinforced by measurable targets and clear accountabilities.

Strategic Pillars

Five priorities that connect our mission to our vision

Each pillar represents a critical area of investment and focus. Together they form an integrated strategy — progress in one area reinforces progress in the others.

01

Digital-First Customer Experience

Invest £2.4 billion over five years in omnichannel infrastructure, personalisation technology, and a unified commerce platform. By 2030, we aim for 45% of revenue to flow through digital channels — up from 28% today — while ensuring physical stores remain vibrant community hubs.

E-commerce Omnichannel Personalisation Technology investment
02

Responsible Sourcing & Circularity

Achieve 100% traceability across Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers by 2028. Launch "Meridian Renewed" — a product take-back and resale programme — in all markets by 2027. Reduce Scope 3 emissions by 40% against our 2022 baseline, aligned with Science Based Targets.

Supply chain Sustainability Circular economy ESG targets
03

Community-Anchored Growth

Expand into 8 new markets across Southeast Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa, prioritising local partnerships, local sourcing, and culturally relevant product ranges. Every new market entry must demonstrate measurable community benefit within 24 months of opening.

International expansion Localisation Community impact
04

People & Culture Investment

Become a top-10 employer in every market we operate in. Deliver 40 hours of development per team member per year, close all gender and ethnicity pay gaps by 2028, and introduce employee equity participation across all regions by 2027.

Talent development DEI Employee engagement Pay equity
05

Operational Excellence & Efficiency

Implement AI-driven demand forecasting to reduce inventory waste by 30%. Transition 100% of own-brand packaging to recyclable or compostable materials by 2029. Achieve a 200-basis-point improvement in operating margin through supply chain optimisation and energy efficiency.
